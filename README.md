## The Control Freak's Dream (In a Good Way)

Here's where Coast gets interesting if you've ever had that sinking feeling when you see a $200 fuel charge at 2 AM from a truck that should be parked.

### What You Can Control

- **Spending limits**: By day, week, or month
- **Merchant categories**: Fuel only? Fuel + maintenance? Your call
- **Location restrictions**: GPS-based controls to prevent fraud
- **Time restrictions**: Because why would anyone need to fuel at 3 AM unless they're actually driving
- **Odometer requirements**: Can mandate odometer readings at purchase
- **Instant virtual cards**: Issue cards to seasonal workers, deactivate when done

The GPS thing is cleverâ€”integrate Coast with telematics like Samsara, Geotab, or Verizon Connect, and transactions get automatically declined if the vehicle isn't actually at the pump. It's like a fraud prevention system that actually prevents fraud instead of just notifying you after the fact.

### Real-Time Everything

Every transaction hits your dashboard immediately. No waiting for statements. No surprises. Just "John fueled up at Shell on I-95, 45 gallons, $3.89/gallon, vehicle odometer 47,832." 

You can set up text alerts too, if you're the type who likes knowing the second something happens (no judgment).

Once approved, cards ship free via standard shipping (5-10 business days). Need them faster? Express shipping is available for a fee.

## Real Talk: Is Coast Worth It?

Look, no fleet card is perfect for everyone. But Coast does a few things really well:

1. **Transparency**: They tell you what things cost upfront, in plain English, on their website. Revolutionary, I know.

2. **Flexibility**: Visa acceptance means drivers aren't route-planning around approved stations, which saves time and fuel.

3. **Modern platform**: The dashboard is actually intuitive. Integrations actually work. Mobile app actually functions. These should be table stakes, but you'd be surprised.

4. **Real-time controls**: Set limits, get alerts, prevent fraud before it happens instead of discovering it three weeks later.

5. **Reasonable pricing**: $4/month per active card is competitive, especially with no transaction fees or hidden charges.

The charge card structure (pay in full each cycle) is either a feature or a bug depending on your cash flow situation. If you can swing it, it encourages good financial discipline. If cash flow is tight, it might stress you out.

Bottom line: If you run a local fleet (5-50 vehicles, mostly gas stations, mix of fuel and non-fuel expenses), Coast is probably worth a serious look. The time you save on expense tracking alone might justify the cost.
